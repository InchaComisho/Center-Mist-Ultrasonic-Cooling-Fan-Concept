## Overview

This document organizes the concept of developing the Center-Mist Ultrasonic Cooling Fan into a PET bottle cap-type product that can connect to widely available bottled-water containers.

The purpose is to treat the 28mm-class PET bottle neck as a low-cost, widely available water-tank interface, and to examine the possibility of integrating a mist-generation unit and a small fan into the upper cap unit.

This page is a conceptual and specification-level discussion. It does not provide hazardous DIY instructions involving electrical work, sealed-container modification, waterproofing, rotating parts, or battery modification.

---

## 1. Compatibility and Limits of 28mm-Class PET Bottles

Many beverage PET bottles use 28mm-class neck finishes. If this interface can be used, locally available bottled water may become the water source for a cooling device without requiring a dedicated tank.

However, the phrase “28mm-class” does not mean perfect global compatibility.

- Thread shapes may differ by region.
- Carbonated-drink bottles, mineral-water bottles, and reusable bottles may have different specifications.
- Gasket shape, tightening depth, neck height, and sealing surface may not match.
- Stability may vary depending on bottle material and wall thickness.
- Safety under heat, direct sunlight, and tipping requires separate verification.

Therefore, this concept treats the 28mm-class PET bottle neck as a global minimum candidate interface, but actual commercialization requires compatibility testing by region and bottle type.

---

## 2. Cap Unit Concept

The PET bottle cap-type unit is considered as a small upper unit attached to the bottle neck.

Conceptual components include:

- cap connection part fixed to the bottle neck,
- water-supply structure to the mist-generation unit,
- ultrasonic mist-generation unit,
- small fan to carry mist on airflow,
- mist outlet,
- sealing structure to reduce tipping and leakage,
- internal separation between water and electrical parts.

The important point is not to pack everything into the cap unit.

If water, airflow, mist, power source, and control board are concentrated inside a small cap area, problems of weight, heat, waterproofing, tipping, and maintenance become serious.

---

## 3. Wick-Based Water Supply Concept

A possible method for sending water from the PET bottle to the mist-generation unit is a wick-based water supply.

A wick system uses absorbent material or capillary structures to draw water from the bottle into the upper unit.

Expected advantages:

- may simplify the structure without a pump,
- may reduce power consumption,
- may reduce noise and failure points,
- may support miniaturization.

Required verification:

- whether the water absorption rate is sufficient for mist generation,
- whether stable supply continues as bottle water decreases,
- whether the wick can be kept hygienic,
- whether mold, slime, and microbial growth can be prevented,
- whether it can be handled safely as a replaceable part.

A wick system is a promising candidate, but hygiene and replaceability must be designed carefully.

---

## 4. Small Fan and Offset-Drive Concept

If a small fan is placed on the cap unit, the ideal center-mist outlet conflicts with the presence of the fan’s central shaft.

There are two possible directions:

1. simple method: introduce mist from the front, rear, or periphery of the small fan;
2. offset-drive method: move the motor sideways or to the outer ring to open the center.

The simple method is easier and lower cost. The offset-drive method approaches ideal center-mist injection, but introduces challenges in rotating-part safety, noise, durability, manufacturing precision, and cost.

For the PET bottle cap type, a realistic roadmap is to first verify safety and marketability with the simple method, and then consider the offset-drive method as a higher-end version.

---

## 5. External Power on the Bottle Body

If the battery is built into the cap unit, the top becomes heavy and the bottle becomes easier to tip. It also concentrates power near water, making waterproofing, insulation, and heat management more difficult.

Therefore, this concept recommends placing the power source not in the cap unit, but externally on the bottle body to lower the center of gravity.

Concept examples:

- external battery band wrapped around the bottle body,
- power module located near the lower part of the bottle,
- waterproof connector separating the cap unit from the power module,
- design that clearly separates charging parts from water paths.

This may lower the center of gravity, reduce tipping risk, and make the cap unit lighter.

Actual products require electrical safety standards, waterproof ratings, overcharge protection, short-circuit protection, heat tests, and drop tests.

## 6. Commercialization Roadmap

This concept can be examined through the following phases.

### Phase 1: Concept Verification

- Regional sampling of 28mm-class bottle necks.
- Basic dimensional organization of the cap connection part.
- Comparison of water-supply methods.
- Target values for mist amount, airflow, and power consumption.

### Phase 2: Safety Design

- Separation between water and electrical parts.
- Evaluation of leakage risk when tipped.
- Organization of mist outlet direction and user distance.
- Warning labels for high-humidity environments.

### Phase 3: Prototype Evaluation

- Attachment tests with multiple bottle types.
- Cooling-effect evaluation under dry-region-like conditions.
- Measurement of WBGT, humidity, wetting, and noise.
- Long-duration hygiene and maintainability checks.

### Phase 4: Regionally Adapted Models

- dry-region model,
- outdoor work-support model,
- disaster-support model,
- educational and experimental model.

### Phase 5: Mass-Production Specification

- clear compatible bottle range,
- standardized replaceable parts,
- compliance with electrical safety standards,
- repair, collection, and recycling design.

## 7. Notes for High-Humidity Environments

Cooling by ultrasonic mist and airflow can produce evaporative cooling in dry environments. In high-humidity environments, however, water evaporates less easily and increased humidity may worsen perceived heat stress or WBGT.

Care is especially needed in:

- poorly ventilated indoor spaces,
- already humid summer environments,
- crowded shelters or waiting spaces,
- places near electronic equipment or paper documents,
- floors where wetness may cause slip accidents.

If commercialized, the product should clearly display humidity-based use limits, warnings, WBGT guidance, and ventilation conditions.

## 8. Use Scenarios in Dry Regions

The PET bottle cap type may be especially useful in dry and semi-arid regions.

Possible applications:

- local cooling for outdoor workers,
- heat-stress reduction during farm work,
- cooling support for street stalls and temporary shops,
- simple heat countermeasures in schools and local facilities,
- disaster support in dry regions,
- low-power cooling during power outages,
- personal cooling while moving or waiting.

Because it may use widely available bottled-water containers, it may have advantages in supply, distribution, and disaster response compared with dedicated-tank devices.

## 10. Safety and Verification Requirements

Because this concept combines water, electricity, rotating parts, mist, and portable use, safety design is the most important requirement.

Minimum verification items include:

- separation of electrical parts and water paths,
- shutdown and protection design in case of leakage,
- waterproof and splash-resistant performance,
- battery overcharge, over-discharge, and short-circuit protection,
- fan guard and finger-pinch prevention,
- safety when tipped,
- stability when attached to bottles,
- wetting around the user,
- safety of mist inhalation,
- water quality, hygiene, and wick replaceability,
- regional bottle compatibility,
- resin deformation and degradation under high temperature.

This page does not recommend dangerous DIY procedures. Implementation should be designed and evaluated by manufacturers, research institutions, municipalities, or experts based on safety standards and verification tests.

## Conclusion

The PET Bottle Cap Edition Center-Mist Ultrasonic Cooling Fan is a concept that uses globally available water bottles as a low-cost water-tank interface.

Key design ideas:

- Treat 28mm-class necks as a minimum common candidate.
- Do not assume perfect compatibility.
- Keep the cap unit lightweight.
- Place power externally on the bottle body to lower the center of gravity.
- Use caution in high-humidity environments.
- Focus on dry regions, outdoor use, disaster support, and local cooling.
- Measurement and risk management are required for Cooling Credit evaluation.

This concept is one direction for developing the Center-Mist Ultrasonic Cooling Fan into a more familiar, more distributable, and more regionally adaptable cooling technology.
